Title: Iron Quantification in Images of Stained Wheat Grains




Abstract: Iron deficiency has a negative impact on about a quarter of the population of the world, sometimes causing severe complications, such as delayed growth in children. One potential solution to this problem is to increase the iron content in widely used staple crops. Of importance to this endeavour is the quick and reliable determination of iron concentration in plants, which can be achieved, for example, through the specific staining of iron-rich tissues using the Perls Prussian blue method. When this method is employed, tissues rich in iron turn blue, with the intensity of the blue colour being proportional to the iron concentration.   

Here we present an image analysis pipeline, written in Python, whose aim is to segment and quantify the colour blue in images of wheat grains stained using the Perls Prussian blue method. The ultimate purpose of the pipeline is to facilitate the selection of genetically improved wheat varieties that have a high iron concentration. Briefly, the pipeline consists in transforming the images into the HSV format, segmenting the colour blue using the hue channel and, finally, quantifying this colour using the saturation channel. The results generated by the analysis pipeline are compared with human-generated scores.
