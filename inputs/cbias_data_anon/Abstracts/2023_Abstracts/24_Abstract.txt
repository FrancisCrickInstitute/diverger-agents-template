Title: Advancing FAIR data sharing for multiplexed tissue imaging data from the Human Tumor Atlas Network



Abstract: Through the creation of detailed molecular and spatial atlases of human tumors, leveraging multiplexed tissue imaging techniques such as CyCIF, CODEX, and MIBI, supplemented by bulk, single-cell, and spatial sequencing, the Human Tumor Atlas Network (HTAN DCC) has paved the way for an in-depth understanding of diverse cancer patterns. To effectively manage the extensive data generated by HTAN atlas programs the HTAN Data Coordinating Center (DCC) was established, adopting the "Minimum Information for Tissue Imaging" (MITI) guidelines for the first time on a large scale. A community-led process helped shape a detailed REMBI-compliant data model, encompassing raw data requirements, harmonized OME-TIFFs, segmentation masks, and feature arrays, enriched by comprehensive participant and biospecimen metadata. The validation and submission of over 800 curated metadata attributes consistently ensure FAIR principles. The HTAN DCC coordinates data curation and sharing via the HTAN Data Portal, guiding users to data access through NCI Cancer Research Data Commons nodes, and facilitating image visualization via automated narrative guides and unsupervised previews. To date, the HTAN DCC has released over 41,000 data files from 20 assays, 6221 biospecimens, and 1546 cases across 48 cancer types to the public. Our ongoing efforts focus on advancing the infrastructure, tools, and community management needed for delivering an effective DCC for cancer cell atlases while exploring innovative ways for the cancer research community to reuse and exploit HTAN's detailed atlases.
