Title: Development of cluster analysis tools for high throughput single molecule localisation microscopy 





Abstract: With single molecule localisation microscopy (SMLM), nanoarchitecture can be interrogated to explore biological mechanisms. We are developing high throughput SMLM instrumentation using easySTORM [1-2] implemented on the open-source openFrame platform [3]. To process large SMLM datasets on a practical timescale, we are working towards HPC-based cluster analysis. Currently, we are exploring two unbiased rapid cluster analysis techniques requiring no user-defined input parameters. The first is a multi-component pair correlation function model-based fitting pipeline that can analyse SMLM data of 100 nuclei in 40 minutes parallelised per nucleus on 12-core HPC nodes (each with 128 GB RAM) and yields similar results to established methods such as DBSCAN with simulated datasets. When applied to high throughput easySTORM data of (H3-AF647-labelled) chromatin structure in ovarian cancer cells treated with platinum chemotherapy, our first results indicate a decompaction of chromatin structure in treatment-resistant cells, which may indicate an underlying epigenetic mechanism driving resistance. The second technique currently utilizes a rapid model-based Bayesian approach to determine the optimum values for cluster radius search and density threshold [4] and can similarly process 100 nuclei in 40 minutes. This preliminary work indicates that high throughput SMLM with such cluster analysis pipelines can become a practical assay modality.
