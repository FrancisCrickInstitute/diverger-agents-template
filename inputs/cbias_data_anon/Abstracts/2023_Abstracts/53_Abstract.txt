Title: Using deep learning for histopathological image classification and detection of signet ring cells in oesophageal adenocarcinoma


Abstract: Deep learning models can detect prognostically significant cells and classify regions of images. This has the potential to automate workflows to decrease pathologist workload. We train fully supervised deep learning models using convolutional neural networks (CNN) to automate digital pathology classification tasks for detection of signet ring cells and grading of oesophageal adenocarcinoma. Signet ring cells are a valuable marker of treatment outcome in oesophageal cancer, and the predictive power of our models trained on oesophageal adenocarcinoma can be transferred to other forms of cancer where detection of these cells is significant such as gastric carcinoma. Using the trained models, we can infer slide-level labels and produce inference maps to visualise and assess regions of interest. We find that our deep learning models can achieve accuracies for detection of signet ring cells and classification of tumour grade comparable with experienced pathologists. We propose that inference maps generated by these highly accurate models can be used alongside human observers for semi-automated workflows. The application of our deep learning models has the potential to enhance the efficiency and accuracy of histopathological analysis, ultimately benefiting patient care and advancing medical research.
