Title: Scaling OMERO for Histopathology and Beyond: Experiences from UCL

Abstract:
The Medical Imaging Research Software Group (MIRSG) within UCL’s Advanced Research Computing (ARC) Centre develops and supports research software and infrastructure for imaging science. A core component of this work is the provision of OMERO as a service, an open-source platform for managing, visualising and analysing microscopy data and metadata. 

At UCL, MIRSG currently operate a dedicated OMERO server for histopathology imaging, enabling researchers to store, share and interrogate large whole-slide images in a secure, centralised repository. This service has supported collaborative projects across multiple groups, providing practical insights into the challenges of running OMERO at scale. Issues around handling terabyte-sized datasets, heterogeneous metadata standards, and integration with downstream analysis pipelines have shaped our approach to both system administration and user support. 

Looking ahead, we are working with researchers to extend OMERO to support larger and more diverse microscopy modalities. Leveraging Condenser, UCL’s new on-prem cloud platform, we aim to design through more resources and improved architecture an OMERO service capable of managing higher-throughput data with potential hooks into computational analysis workflows. By sharing our experiences of maintaining a production histopathology OMERO and outlining our plans for expansion, we hope to contribute to community discussions on sustainable, analysis-ready microscopy data infrastructure.
